Process Map or Customer Journey Map?

In order to receive or maintain a ISO 9001 certification businesses have to define, describe and document all processes of their organization. Most ISO 9001 certified companies use a so called "process map" to provide a comprehensive, visualized overview of all processes. This process map helps all of their employees, better understand how a process oriented quality management system works. Typically, process maps divide in the following three units:

• Management Processes (e.g. Strategy)
• Operating Processes (e.g. Sourcing; hint: can also be a “customer journey map”
• Supporting Processes (e.g. Quality Assurance)

On the process map, all company processes are assigned to one of these three categories. Together with 50+ quality management consultants, we derived a process map template that can be used for any kind of company that reflects your individual organizational structure with only a few changes. With leanquo, the process map will be established at the core of your organization. Every employee / colleague is now able to access leanquo from PC, tablet or smartphone.

With integrated documented information & persons in charge.

Once the process map is at the heart of your organization, with leanquo you simply link documented information and persons in charge to each of your process buttons. The admin (e.g. quality manager) just clicks on one of the process buttons and via drag & drop the documented information can be uploaded within the respective process. leanquo adds all process #keywords automatically, so you and your colleagues find these documents in an easy way. Secondly, you can choose persons in charge for each process. These will be shown on the bottom of the list. So if a colleague clicks on a process, she will not only find all important documented information, but also the colleagues in charge. Once a documented information is outdated, you can archive it with one click. The moment you upload a documented information, you can assign the author to this document. With leanquo, you control who accesses which documented information within your organisation, as lean and agile as it gets.
